# iPad and iPhone Kung Fu

## Key Concepts

This book reveals hidden features, shortcuts, and power-user techniques for iOS devices beyond obvious surface-level interactions, teaching users to work more efficiently and customize devices to their preferences. The core philosophy treats iOS not as locked-down appliance but as powerful platform with depth of features discoverable through exploration, keyboard shortcuts, gestures, and settings buried beneath defaults. Key techniques include mastering multi-finger gestures for navigation and multitasking, using keyboard shortcuts for text entry and formatting, and leveraging built-in apps beyond obvious use cases like Reminders for location-based alerts or Notes for document scanning. The book covers customizing control center and notification center for quick access to frequently used features, using accessibility features even for users without disabilities like AssistiveTouch providing on-screen home button or VoiceOver for eyes-free interaction, and automating workflows through Shortcuts app chaining actions across apps without manual repetition.

## Who Should Read It and Why

This book targets iOS users who use devices primarily through obvious taps wanting to discover hidden gestures and features making interactions faster, productivity enthusiasts seeking every efficiency gain from devices used daily for work and personal tasks, and tech-savvy users who know Android power features wondering what iOS offers beyond surface simplicity. Those who switched from Android to iOS and feel limited by apparent simplicity will discover powerful capabilities hidden beneath clean interface. The content particularly benefits professionals using iPads as laptop replacements needing keyboard shortcuts, external monitor support, and file management approaching desktop capabilities, along with users frustrated by slow typing wanting text replacement shortcuts, one-handed keyboard mode, or dictation features. Anyone who has watched power users navigate iOS impossibly fast or discovered features by accident and wondered what else they're missing will appreciate systematic exploration of device capabilities.

## Practical Applications

Readers will learn to navigate using gestures like swiping between apps, pinching to home screen, or swiping up for multitasking instead of repeatedly pressing home button, type faster using text replacement for frequently used phrases, dictation for hands-free input, or third-party keyboards offering swiping or different layouts, and customize control center adding shortcuts to flashlight, calculator, camera, or other frequently accessed features. The book demonstrates productivity features like split screen and slide over for working with multiple apps simultaneously on iPad, picture-in-picture for video playback while using other apps, and widgets providing glanceable information without opening apps. Practical tips cover Safari power-user features like tab groups organizing sites, reader view for distraction-free reading, and content blockers preventing ads and trackers, Photos management using albums, smart albums with automatic organization, and markup tools for annotating images, and Messages capabilities like tapback responses, mentions in group chats, and pinned conversations keeping important threads accessible. Advanced techniques include using Shortcuts app creating custom automations triggered by time, location, or NFC tags, leveraging Handoff continuing tasks from iPhone to Mac or iPad seamlessly, and using iCloud Drive and Files app for cross-device document access with folder organization. The book provides tips for battery life optimization understanding what drains power and adjusting settings, security and privacy hardening through two-factor authentication, app permissions review, and Find My device tracking, and troubleshooting common problems from frozen apps to connection issues. Hidden features covered include using iPad as second display for Mac, scanning documents directly in Notes or Files, measuring objects with camera using AR, and transcribing voicemails automatically without listening.
